Theory Section

This section presents my exploration of various theories relevant to my project, laying the groundwork for the design decisions I made.

Essay: Theoretical Foundations in Design

Design theories have been instrumental in shaping the user experience throughout this project. One of the key theories I've applied is the Gestalt Principles, which focus on how users perceive visual elements as a whole rather than in parts. By applying concepts like proximity and similarity, I was able to create cohesive groupings of related information, leading to a more intuitive navigation experience for users.

Preparatory Work

In preparation for my project, I delved into existing UI/UX frameworks and guidelines, including studying established design systems such as Material Design and Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. This research guided my decisions around color schemes, typography, and layouts, ensuring the website maintained consistency and user-friendliness throughout.

Reflections

Reflecting on the design process, I found that balancing aesthetics with functionality was one of the most challenging aspects. Initially, my designs leaned more toward visual appeal, but after user testing, I realized that some areas needed adjustments to improve usability. These adjustments highlighted the importance of prioritizing clarity and ease of navigation, which ultimately improved the overall user experience.

UI/UX Decisions

This section outlines the UI/UX design choices I made during the project, which were guided by the theories discussed in the previous section.

Introduction to Specter & Co Solutions

Inspired by the character Harvey Specter from the TV show "Suits," my goal was to create a high-end legal consultancy website that exudes professionalism and confidence. I wanted to capture the essence of Harvey Specter's expertise and reputation in the visual identity of the website.

Colour Choices

For this project, I carefully selected the following colors to reinforce the website's professional tone:

  • Navy Blue (#002B5C)
  • Deep Gray (#4A4A4A)
  • Light Gray (#E5E5E5)
  • White (#FFFFFF)

Pop Colours: These colors are used to draw attention to important buttons and UI elements:

  • Teal (#008080)
  • Crimson Red (#DC143C)

Commercial Website Style Guide

UI Elements – Colour Explanation

My color palette ensures enough contrast between text, backgrounds, and forms to maintain readability while effectively communicating important information:

  • Navy Blue (#002B5C): Used for headers, navigation bars, primary buttons, and backgrounds.
  • Deep Gray (#4A4A4A): Used for secondary buttons, borders, and text elements.
  • Light Gray (#E5E5E5): Used for background sections, input fields, and form areas.
  • White (#FFFFFF): Used for text on dark backgrounds and as the main background color in specific sections.
  • Crimson Red (#DC143C): Used for critical buttons, calls to action, and key highlights.
  • Teal (#008080): Used for secondary accents, icons, and less prominent buttons.

Typography

Typography was crucial in establishing the professional and confident tone I wanted to convey:

  • Font Family: I chose Helvetica, a modern sans-serif font with a neutral, clean appearance. It’s a font that reflects confidence and professionalism, used by brands like BMW and American Airlines.
  • Line and Letter Spacing: To enhance readability and maintain a polished look, I selected the following font sizes:
    • 22px to 26px for body text.
    • 28px to 34px for headings.
    • 23px to 27px for subheadings.
    • 17px to 20px for navigation and UI elements.

Style Guide

To maintain consistency across the website, I developed a style guide that includes:

  • Color Palette: Featuring Navy Blue (#002B5C) and Teal (#008080) for branding, with Light Gray (#E5E5E5) for background accents.
  • Typography: Body text ranging from 22px to 26px for readability, and headings from 28px to 34px to establish hierarchy.
  • Button Styles: Primary buttons have rounded corners, solid background colors, and contrasting text for better visibility.
